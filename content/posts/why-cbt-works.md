Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is based on the idea that our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are interconnected. It is a highly structured, goal-oriented form of psychotherapy that focuses on the here and now rather than diving exclusively into childhood history.

## The CBT Triangle

At the heart of CBT is the "Cognitive Triangle." This model suggests that:

1.  **Thoughts**: What we think affects how we feel and act.
2.  **Feelings**: How we feel affects what we think and do.
3.  **Behaviors**: What we do affects how we think and feel.

By identifying and challenging negative thought patterns ("I'm going to fail"), we can change our emotional response (anxiety) and our behavior (avoidance).

> "The greatest discovery of my generation is that a human being can alter his life by altering his attitudes of mind." — William James

## Evidence-Based Results

Research consistently shows that CBT is as effective as medication for many anxiety disorders, with longer-lasting results. Unlike medication, which manages symptoms, CBT provides a **toolkit of skills** that patients can use for the rest of their lives.

### Why it works for anxiety:

- **Cognitive Restructuring**: Learning to identify "cognitive distortions" like catastrophizing.
- **Exposure Therapy**: Gradually facing fears in a safe, controlled environment.
- **Skill Building**: Developing immediate coping mechanisms for panic or stress.

Research shows that CBT is as effective as medication for many anxiety disorders, with longer-lasting results. By committing to the practice, users can literally rewire their brain's response to stress.
