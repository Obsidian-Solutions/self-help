Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is based on the idea that our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are interconnected.

By identifying and challenging negative thought patterns ("I'm going to fail"), we can change our emotional response (anxiety) and our behavior (avoidance).

Research shows that CBT is as effective as medication for many anxiety disorders, with longer-lasting results.
